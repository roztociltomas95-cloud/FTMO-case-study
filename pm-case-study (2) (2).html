Background

The Opportunity

FTMO is one of the world's leading proprietary trading firms — funding traders who prove their edge through a structured evaluation programme. We are now expanding beyond financial markets and stepping into sports prediction markets.

The idea is simple in principle, ambitious in execution: replicate the FTMO model — challenge, evaluation, funding — but for sports. Participants go through a challenge, get funded, and use that capital to participate in sports prediction markets. It's FTMO's DNA applied to a new arena.

Critically, this will be built as a separate, independent brand. You have access to FTMO's infrastructure, operational know-how, technology, and resources — but you are starting from a blank sheet of paper on brand, product, and go-to-market. The reason for this separation is deliberate: FTMO is perceived as a platform for serious, disciplined traders. Sports prediction markets sit much closer to gambling in the public consciousness, and we don't want that association to bleed into the core FTMO brand. The new venture needs to stand entirely on its own — earning its own trust, its own audience, and its own identity.

⚡ Your mandate

Imagine you are the founding PM of this new venture. You have FTMO's backing — their tech stack, compliance expertise, funding model, and operational muscle. Now design, from scratch, what this sports prediction markets product looks like: the brand, the product experience, the user journey, the business model, the market entry strategy, and how you'd build a team around it.

The Case Study

What You'll Present

Design a sports prediction markets venture from scratch — new brand, FTMO's infrastructure behind it. The brief is intentionally open-ended. There is no right answer, no expected structure, and no required coverage. What we want to understand is how you think about building a business: what you prioritise, how you connect product decisions to commercial ones, and where you'd put your energy first. The four areas below are prompts to help you get started, not a template to fill in.

💡 A note on format

Bring whatever tells your story best — slides, a Figma board, a one-pager, a live prototype. Depth on a few areas beats shallow coverage of everything.

What we look for in your presentation

We aren't scoring a written exam. We look for a sharp point of view on the space, connected thinking where product, brand, and business decisions reinforce each other, a bias for action — knowing what to do first and why — and honest acknowledgement of risk. The candidates who stand out don't cover everything; they make us believe they can lead this.
